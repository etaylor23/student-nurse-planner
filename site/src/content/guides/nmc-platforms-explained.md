The seven platforms are how the NMC organises the [219 proficiency statements](/guides/nmc-proficiencies-and-your-pad) every registered nurse must meet — seven broad dimensions of nursing, from professional accountability to coordinating care. Understanding what each platform *means in practice* makes your PAD less of a checklist and more of a map: once you can recognise a platform in the middle of an ordinary shift, evidence stops being something you hunt for and starts being something you notice.

## What a "platform" actually is

Each platform is a themed group of proficiency statements in the NMC's [*Standards of proficiency for registered nurses*](https://www.nmc.org.uk/standards/standards-for-nurses/standards-of-proficiency-for-registered-nurses/). The statements within it describe specific knowledge, skills and behaviours; together, the seven platforms (plus Annexe A's communication skills and Annexe B's procedures) define what "registered nurse" means, in every field of practice.

Your programme assesses you against them through your PAD, so the platforms are also the structure your sign-offs follow.

## The seven platforms, in plain English

**1. Being an accountable professional.** Practising honestly, ethically and within the Code; knowing your limits and owning your decisions. On placement this is the everyday fabric — introducing yourself and your student status, escalating when unsure, keeping confidentiality (including in your [reflections](/guides/how-to-write-a-nursing-reflection)).

**2. Promoting health and preventing ill health.** Supporting people to protect and improve their own health. It looks like brief conversations that stick: smoking, alcohol, vaccination, screening, activity — plus understanding the wider determinants behind them.

**3. Assessing needs and planning care.** Taking histories, observations and assessments, and turning them into person-centred plans. Every admission assessment, every care-plan review, every deteriorating-patient recognition sits here.

**4. Providing and evaluating care.** The delivery of nursing care and — the part students under-evidence — *judging whether it worked*. Wound care, medicines, nutrition, comfort, and the evaluation and re-planning that follow.

**5. Leading and managing nursing care and working in teams.** Delegation, supervision, teamwork and coordination-in-miniature. As a senior student this becomes managing a bay, supervising a junior student, or leading a handover.

**6. Improving safety and quality of care.** Patient safety, risk assessment, incident reporting, audit and quality improvement. Noticing the near-miss and knowing what happens after the datix is platform 6 territory.

**7. Coordinating care.** Care across teams, settings and transitions — discharge planning, referrals, working with families and other agencies, continuity for people with complex needs.

## Using the platforms as a lens on an ordinary shift

Take one unremarkable morning: you take handover (5), do the observations round and escalate a NEWS2 score (3, 6), give supervised medications (4), have a two-minute conversation about smoking cessation (2), coordinate with the discharge team (7), and log it all honestly, asking for help once when out of your depth (1). One shift, six platforms.

That's the practical trick: **don't hunt for special "proficiency moments" — learn to name the platforms in the work you're already doing.** Capture the moment briefly on the day and link it to the right statements later; the [evidence guide](/guides/how-to-evidence-nmc-proficiencies) walks through the exact system, and [PlaceMate](https://app.placemate.uk) tracks all 219 statements platform-by-platform so you can see your coverage at a glance.

## Where the annexes fit

Two lists sit alongside the platforms: **Annexe A** (communication and relationship management skills) and **Annexe B** (nursing procedures, in 11 groups). Think of the platforms as the *what and why* of nursing and the annexes as the *how* — the concrete skills and procedures the platforms are exercised through. More in the [proficiencies and PAD pillar guide](/guides/nmc-proficiencies-and-your-pad).

## Frequently asked questions

**How many platforms are there in the NMC standards?**
Seven, plus Annexe A (communication and relationship management skills) and Annexe B (nursing procedures). Together they contain the 219 proficiency statements.

**Are the platforms assessed separately?**
Your PAD organises assessment around them, but real practice crosses platforms constantly — one well-chosen piece of evidence often supports statements in several platforms at once.

**Which platform should I focus on first?**
None exclusively — but platform 1 (accountable professional) underpins everything and is where first-year evidence naturally accumulates. Leadership-heavy statements in platform 5 tend to come into their own in later placements.

**Do the platforms differ by field of nursing?**
No — the same platforms and statements apply across adult, mental health, learning disabilities and children's nursing. What differs is the context you evidence them in.
