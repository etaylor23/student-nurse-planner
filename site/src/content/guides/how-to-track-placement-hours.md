The reliable way to track placement hours is to **log every shift on the day you work it** — date, placement, hours worked, and whether they're real or simulated — then reconcile against your rota once a week. It takes under a minute a shift, and it's the difference between always knowing where you stand with the [2,300-hour requirement](/guides/student-nurse-placement-hours-explained) and reconstructing a year of shifts from old rotas in third year.

## What to record for every shift

Whatever tool you use, capture the same five things:

1. **The date** — obvious, but it's what makes your log auditable against the rota later.
2. **The placement** — hours are usually reviewed per placement, not just in total.
3. **Hours worked** — your actual worked hours, following your programme's rules on breaks (some count them, most don't; check your handbook once and apply it consistently).
4. **Real or simulated** — simulated practice hours count [only up to the 600-hour cap](/guides/student-nurse-placement-hours-explained), so the split matters from day one.
5. **Anything unusual** — left early, stayed late, sent home, off sick. These are the entries you'll be glad you annotated when someone queries a discrepancy.

That's the whole record. The discipline is not in the detail — it's in doing it *on the day*, while the shift is still fresh and the rota is still pinned to the fridge.

## Paper, spreadsheet, or app?

All three work. They fail in different ways.

**Paper** is fast and always available, but it can't add up for you, and one lost notebook is a lost year. If you use paper, photograph the pages regularly.

**A spreadsheet** adds the arithmetic — running totals, a remaining-hours cell, even a simulated-hours column with a cap check. Its weakness is friction: opening a laptop after a night shift is where most spreadsheet systems quietly die.

## The weekly reconcile: two minutes that save hours

Once a week — end of your last shift, or Sunday evening — check your log against the rota:

- Every rostered shift has an entry.
- The hours match what you actually worked.
- Sickness or absence is recorded as a **deficit**, not silently skipped.

Small errors caught within a week are trivial to fix; the same errors found at the end of a placement mean chasing a ward clerk for months-old rotas. Two minutes, weekly, non-negotiable.

## Handling missed shifts and make-up hours

When you miss hours, record three things: the shift you missed, the size of the deficit, and — when you make it up — which shift cleared it. Universities almost always require missed hours to be made up, and the students who struggle are rarely the ones with deficits; they're the ones who can't say *how big* the deficit is. A visible number is a plannable number.

## The three ways tracking systems fail

1. **Batch logging.** "I'll enter the fortnight on Sunday" becomes guesswork by Tuesday. Log on the day.
2. **Ignoring the simulated split.** If skills-lab hours land in the same bucket as placement hours, you can't prove where you stand against the 600-hour cap when your university asks.
3. **Tracking only the total.** 1,400 hours banked means little if you can't show the per-placement breakdown your programme reviews. Track by placement; the total takes care of itself.

## Frequently asked questions

**When should I log my hours?**
On the day you work the shift, ideally straight after handover. Same-day logging is accurate; everything else is memory.

**Do I include my breaks?**
Follow your programme handbook — most programmes count worked hours excluding unpaid breaks. Decide once, apply consistently, and note the rule in your log.

**What if my log and my PAD disagree?**
Your PAD (and your university's official record) wins. Treat your own log as the early-warning system that stops discrepancies reaching the PAD in the first place.

**How do I track hours across multiple placements?**
Keep one running log with a placement field, and review per placement as well as in total. That's the view your programme assesses — and it's automatic in [PlaceMate](https://app.placemate.uk).
