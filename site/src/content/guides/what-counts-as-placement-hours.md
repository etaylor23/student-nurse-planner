Broadly: **rostered placement shifts count** — days, nights, weekends, bank holidays — along with programme-designated simulated learning (up to 600 hours) and structured learning arranged as part of a placement, such as spoke days. **Travel, sickness, unrostered goodwill time and paid work don't.** The final word on every edge case belongs to your university, but here's the map most programmes follow.

## The golden rule first

Your university defines what counts, in your programme handbook and Practice Assessment Document — and its definition beats anything you read online, including this guide. What follows is the consistent shape across UK programmes, so you know what to expect and which questions to ask. When a case is unclear, ask your practice assessor or placement office *at the time*, not at the end of the year.

## What normally counts

- **Rostered shifts on placement** — whatever the pattern. Nights, weekends, long days and bank holidays are all practice learning; there's no "unsocial hours discount" in either direction. Handover is part of the shift.
- **Simulated practice learning** — skills labs, simulation suites and structured scenario teaching that your programme designates as practice hours. These count toward the 2,300 **up to the 600-hour cap**; the [placement hours pillar guide](/guides/student-nurse-placement-hours-explained) covers how the cap works.
- **Spoke days and insight visits** — days spent with another team (theatres, district nursing, specialist clinics) *arranged as part of your placement* are normally practice hours. Log them under the host placement.
- **Structured learning within the placement** — teaching sessions, supervised clinics and similar activities that happen inside your rostered time.

## What normally doesn't count

- **Travel** — the commute to placement, however long, is not practice learning. (A long commute is worth raising with your placement team for other reasons; it just doesn't earn hours.)
- **Sickness and absence** — missed hours don't count and usually must be **made up** before the end of the programme. Record the deficit when it happens so it never becomes a surprise; [our tracking guide](/guides/how-to-track-placement-hours) covers how.
- **Unrostered extra time** — staying two hours past your shift out of goodwill doesn't add practice hours unless your programme explicitly counts it. Know the rule before you rely on it.
- **Paid work** — bank HCA shifts and other employment don't count toward practice hours, no matter how clinically relevant. The NMC requirement is for *supervised practice learning within your programme*.
- **Private study** — revision, assignment writing and e-learning are theory-side unless your programme says otherwise.

## The edge cases worth asking about

A few genuinely vary between universities — flag these with your programme rather than assuming:

- **Breaks** — most programmes count worked hours excluding unpaid breaks, but check.
- **Trust induction and orientation days** — often counted, not always.
- **Interprofessional learning days** during placement blocks — designation varies.
- **Being sent home early** (low acuity, ward moves) — usually you log the hours actually worked; some programmes have specific rules.

The pattern in all of them: **when the answer affects your total, get it in writing once** — then apply it consistently in your log.

## Why this matters more in third year

Any single grey-area day is trivial — 7.5 hours either way. But a programme's worth of miscounted breaks, uncounted spoke days or silently skipped sick days compounds into a shortfall you discover exactly when you can least afford it: at the point your university confirms your hours to the NMC. Students rarely fall short because they didn't work enough; they fall short because they didn't *count* right. ## Frequently asked questions

**Do night shifts count toward placement hours?**
Yes — a rostered night shift is practice learning like any other shift, and so are weekends and bank holidays.

**Do spoke days count as placement hours?**
Normally yes, when arranged as part of your placement. Log them under the host placement and check your programme's rule if unclear.

**Does working as a bank HCA count toward my hours?**
No. Paid employment is separate from your programme's supervised practice learning, however relevant the experience.

**Do simulation hours count?**
Yes — up to 600 of your 2,300 practice hours may be simulated learning designated by your programme. Beyond the cap, hours must come from real placements.

**What happens to hours I miss through sickness?**
They don't count and typically must be made up. Record the deficit at the time and agree with your university how and when you'll clear it.
